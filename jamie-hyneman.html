    Jamie Hyneman Biography

    Jamie Hyneman was born on September 25, 1956, in Marshall, Michigan, USA. Hyneman was a troublemaking adolescent who ran away from home at 14 and traveled across the country. Hyneman attended Indiana University and majored in Russian Linguistics.

    Villanova University also bestowed upon him the honorary degree of Doctor of Engineering. He received an Honorary Doctorate from the University of Twente for his contributions to the popularization of science and technology.

    Career

    Hyneman had almost every job under the sun, including machinist, divemaster, boat captain, wilderness survival expert, linguist, animal wrangler, concrete inspector, chef, and pet store owner. However, once he left his house, Hyneman took over these jobs; he excelled in special effects work, which led to the creation of MythBusters. In the mid-1980s, he made his debut in the effects industry.

    He’s also worked as an animatronics technician on films like Naked Lunch, Flubber, and The Matrix Trilogy. M5 Industries, his special effects company in San Francisco, is owned by him. In 2006, Hyneman attended The Amazing Meeting, an annual skeptics conference. He also made a cameo appearance alongside Savage on CSI: Crime Scene Investigation on May 1, 2008, in the episode “The Theory of Everything.” In 2016, he worked as a contributor and editor at Tested.com with Adam Savage and Norman Chan.

    Achievements and Awards

    Hyneman was known as Dr. H.C. Jamie Hyneman after receiving an honorary doctorate from the University of Twente on its 50th anniversary on November 25, 2011. Hyneman delivered the commencement address and was awarded an honorary Doctor of Engineering degree by Villanova University. He received his honorary doctorate alongside Adam Savage for their work in popularizing science.
