    Larry Hagman’s Early Life

    Larry Hagman was born on September 21, 1931, in Fort Worth, Texas. His mother, Mary Martin, was a Broadway actress, and his father, Benjamin Jack Hagman, was a district attorney. Hagman’s parents divorced when he was five, and he moved to Los Angeles to live with his grandmother. His early exposure to the world of entertainment through his mother played a significant role in shaping his career.

    Beginning of Larry Hagman’s Career

    Hagman began his acting career in the 1950s, performing in various stage productions. His first significant break came in 1965 when he was cast as Major Anthony Nelson in the popular television series “I Dream of Jeannie.” The show was a massive hit and catapulted Hagman to fame.

    Peak of Larry Hagman’s Career

    The peak of Hagman’s career came in the late 1970s when he was cast as J.R. Ewing in the television series “Dallas.” His portrayal of the ruthless oil tycoon earned him critical acclaim and made him a household name. The show ran for 14 seasons and significantly contributed to Hagman’s wealth.

    Other Ventures

    Apart from acting, Hagman also ventured into production. He directed several episodes of “Dallas” and “I Dream of Jeannie,” which added to his income. He also made investments in real estate, which proved to be profitable.

    Larry Hagman’s Net Worth Over the Years

    Posthumous Increase in Net Worth

    Estimated Net Worth in 2024
