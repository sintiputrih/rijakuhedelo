    Kelly Clarkson’s next record finally has a release date and tracklist, and you’ll never guess who’s on one of the songs.

    In a post on social media on Friday (April 14), the three-time Grammy winner said that her highly anticipated record after her divorce, Chemistry, will come out this summer. She also showed off the album’s airy cover art and confirmed that Steve Martin is a guest artist on the project.

    We now know that the album will come out on June 23. It will have two sides and 14 tracks. Two of those tracks, “Mine” and “Me,” were also released Friday.

    Do you know how much money Kelly Clarkson has? How much did she get paid for her appearance on “The voice”:

    Billboard has revealed that Martin is the well-known actor Steve Martin, and he will be on track 13, “I Hate Love.” On track 14, “that’s right,” the famous drummer Sheila E. is the only other person named as having worked on the set.

    Clarkson wrote of the record in a statement –

    “Having chemistry with someone is an incredible, and overwhelming, feeling.”

    “It’s like you have no choice in the matter. You are just drawn to each other. This can be good and bad. This album takes you down every path that chemistry could lead you down. There are many stages of grief and loss on this album. Each song is a different stage and emotional state.”

    The singer of “Since U Been Gone” poses on the cover with her hands up and ghostly prints of herself layered over the picture.

    Clarkson also put out two songs from the record on Friday: “Mine” and “Me.” Gayle helped write “Me,” which was one of the two songs.

    She added in her statement –

    “We decided to release ‘mine’ and ‘me’ at the same time because I didn’t want to release just one song to represent an entire album, or relationship.”
