    Trouble DTE, a rapper from Atlanta, is said to have died at the age of 35. According to several reports, the Atlanta rapper, real name Mariel Semonté Orr, was shot and killed early Sunday morning (June 5). His sister and ex-girlfriend, Alexis Skyy, both confirmed the terrible news. She posted a tweet from Trouble, which he sent only a couple of weeks after she found out:

    “Life is too short, dawg smh.” You never know when your numba will be called! Move with elegance and wit. Be thankful. Don’t forget to forgive. I adore you all. Respect for life! Any day might be all ova! On Lord.”

    Authorities have issued an arrest warrant for Jamichael Jones, 33, who is suspected of committing the killing. Meanwhile, social media is flooded with condolences for the musician. Mike WILL Made-It, who signed Trouble to EarDrummers Entertainment in 2017, was one among many who lamented his death.

    More information about the murder of rapper Trouble

    Jamichael Jones, 33, of Atlanta, was sought on murder, home invasion, and aggravated assault warrants, according to Sheriff Canty. According to Canty, Jones, the suspect, was embroiled in a “domestic issue” with the lady Trouble was visiting, and he and Trouble never met. According to Canty, he stormed into the woman’s house and started a fire, striking Trouble in the chest once. He was declared dead on the scene. The lady, who has not been identified, is not thought to be upset.

    Although no arrests have been made, the Rockdale County Sheriff’s Office has secured warrants in connection with the murder for a suspect identified as 33-year-old Jamichael Jones of Atlanta. Jones is sought on charges of aggravated assault, criminal homicide, and home invasion. According to officials, he is believed to be in the Atlanta and Jonesboro, Georgia, regions. At a news conference on Sunday evening, spokesman Jedidia Canty said the shooting seemed to be a domestic issue. She continued by saying that Trouble and Jones knew the lady they were seeing at the apartment complex.

    The late rapper’s legacy

    On November 4, 1987, Mariel Semonté Orr, also known as Trouble, was born in Atlanta, Georgia. He has always been a fan of hip-hop. He’s always wanted to be a great superstar and show his friends and family that he can work hard and accomplish his objectives no matter what.

    He started rapping at an early age. When he was fourteen, he got interested in hip-hop. DTE’s music career lasted a decade, starting in April 2011 with the release of his mixtape on December 17th. He started his professional career as a rapper in 2011. His net worth is projected to have increased to $1 million during the previous 11 years. Throughout his career, he has amassed a large number of names, renown, and fortune. He created a lot of enemies in the process.

    He released his debut mixtape on December 17, 2011, and it scored No. 23 on Complex magazine’s list of The 25 Best Mixtapes of 2011. The song Bussin was remixed by rappers Yo Gotti, Waka Flocka Flame, and Trae tha Truth for the mixtape. In 2015, he co-wrote the song Chopper alongside Glasses Malone, Trae tha Truth, Billy Blue, Buk, and Fam-Lay for Lupe Fiasco’s album Tetsuo & Youth. In 2016, he appeared on YFN Lucci’s song Key to the Streets, which reached number 70 on the Billboard Hot 100.

    However, in March 2016, rapper and label owner Veli Sosa shot a music video for Straight Out off his 2016 mixtape Skoobzilla Kanye Like A starring Reese DaGreat in North Carolina. He was a husband. In 2017, he gave birth to a son. He was arrested on narcotics charges. He was freed in 2018, and the following year he bought a house for his parents.
