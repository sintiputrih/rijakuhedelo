    Biography of Sylvester Stallone

    On July 6, 1946, Sylvester Stallone was born in New York City, New York, in the United States.

    Stallone’s facial nerve was severed when he was taken from his mother’s womb at the moment of his birth, which is why he has a droopy face and slurred speech.

    His parents’ rocky marriage caused him to spend his first few years of life in foster care. He was reunited with his family at the age of 5, and a few years later his parents divorced. Stallone has academic difficulties as a child and endured multiple expulsions.

    After graduating from a high school for at-risk adolescents, Stallone went on to study acting at The University of Miami and an American College in Switzerland

    Sylvester Stallone Career

    Stallone left The University of Miami before receiving his degree and relocated to New York City to start an acting career. Stallone worked as a theater usher, zookeeper, and performed in a few small roles in low-profile movies while he waited for his career to take off.

    When Stallone wrote his screenplay about Rocky, a budding boxer from Philadelphia, his career really took off. Later, he discovered two producers, Irwin Winkler and Robert Chartoff, who agreed to make his movie and give him the lead role.

    The movie was a huge hit, grossing over $117 million at the box office. Following soon after were Rocky parts 2-3 and Rambo parts 1-3, all of which exceeded expectations.

    Even though his acting career did take a slight downturn after that, with a few underwhelming movies in the middle of the 1990s, Stallone didn’t let that stop him from succeeding.

    Stallone has recently made a comeback in his more recent movies, including The Expendables and Creed. He is now acknowledged on a global scale for having given some of history’s most iconic performances.
