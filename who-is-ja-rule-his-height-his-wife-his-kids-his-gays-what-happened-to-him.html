    In the list of greatest rappers in the world, there is pretty much one reason why Ja Rule would be missing. With a very strong voice, he was among the biggest voices in the rap industry in the early 2000s, before eventually losing the game due to the many clashes he got involved in. Apart from being a rapper, Ja Rule is also a record producer and actor.

    Who is Ja Rule? Biography

    While it’s like Ja rules that much of the world knows the rapper. He was born on February 29, 1976 in Queens, New York as Jeffrey Bruce Atkins. His father, William Atkins, left the family when he was still a child and the responsibility of raising him was left to his mother, Debra.

    His mother lost a child when he was still in her womb due to breathing difficulties. Atkins was therefore raised without a Jehovah’s Witness – a religious organization that his mother would later leave.

    By the age of 12, Jeffrey Atkins had already started selling drugs in Queens to his friend’s family. He started dealing drugs after having to leave his grandparents’ home and live with his mother, who was also struggling at the time.

    What would get him off the streets where he was The rap he started in 1993 was trying to make a living for himself. His first job was with the hip-hop group Cash Money Click, which also had Chris Black and O-1. Her debut album, Venni Vetti Vecci was released in 1999. It became a hit in no time, reaching platinum and paving the way for her next album, 3:36 Rule released in 2000, which outperformed the first.

    Pain is Love is his next album, released in 2001. This became another very successful album which outsold those before. The single, Always On Time which featured Ashanti became one of the most popular songs from the album which was nominated for Best Rap Album at the 2002 Grammy Awards.

    His next albums were The Last Temptation (2002), Blood in My Eyes (2003), and RULE. (2004). He would do other releases, but nothing was as successful as his previous albums.

    Among his first major feud was with 50 Cent and the feud would turn into a beef with the entire G-Unit. In 2002, 50 Cent signed with Shady Records and Aftermath and the move angered Ja Rule who threatened. He attacked Dr. Dre, Eminem and Eminem’s daughter, Hailie Jade, and many believe that his constant feuds with other rappers led to his downfall in the music industry.
